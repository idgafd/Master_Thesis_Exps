\chapter{Supplementary Diagnostics and Bidirectional Comparison}\label{app:diagnostics}

This appendix collects diagnostic figures whose chapter-level
references appear in the synthesis section of
Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}, the chunked-streaming evaluation in
its per-architecture form, and the empirical justification for the
choice of the LION mechanism-level wrapper over the operator-level
bidirectional alternatives. The data underlying every figure is
stored alongside the figure script in the thesis figure folder.

\section{MSDC trained mixing weights}\label{app:diagnostics:msdc}

The MSDC mechanism's per-layer per-channel mixing weights
$\alpha_{d}$ for $d \in \{1, 2, 4, 8\}$ determine whether the
multi-dilation structure is exercised by the gradient. The values
are initialised at $\alpha_{1} = 1$ and $\alpha_{d > 1} = 0.01$ per
the design notes of Section~\ref{sec:proposed:msdc}; trained values
that diverge from initialisation indicate that the multi-dilation
branches receive gradient and contribute to the input-side mixing.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/chapter5/F8_msdc_alpha_heatmaps.pdf}
\caption[MSDC trained $\alpha_{d}$ heatmaps]{Per-layer trained
$\alpha_{d}$ values across the three architectures at the 7M scale.
Rows index encoder layers; columns index dilations
$\{1, 2, 4, 8\}$. The trained $\alpha_{d > 1}$ values diverge from
their small initialisation in every layer of every backbone,
confirming that the multi-dilation branches are exercised by the
gradient and contribute non-trivially to the input-side mixing.}
\label{fig:app:msdc-alpha}
\end{figure}

\section{DHO rotation angle and viscosity diagnostics}\label{app:diagnostics:dho}

The DHO mechanism's per-block rotation angle $\theta_{t, h, b}$ and
its per-head per-block viscosity coefficient $\eta_{h, b}$ are the
two diagnostic scalars that determine whether the mechanism's
physical-prior structure is exercising. The viscosity is
initialised at $\eta_{h, b} = 0$, and trained values that diverge
from zero indicate that the Rayleigh dissipation coupling engages.
The rotation angle is bounded by the depth-graded clip schedule of
Section~\ref{sec:proposed:dho}.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/chapter5/F9_dho_eta_theta.pdf}
\caption[DHO $\eta$ and $\theta$ diagnostics]{Per-layer rotation-angle
and viscosity-coefficient diagnostics for the DHO cells in the
matrix. The depth-graded $\theta$-clip schedule is visible in the
realised distribution of $\theta$; the trained $\eta$ values
exhibit architecture-specific patterns that align with each
backbone's structural deficit.}
\label{fig:app:dho-eta-theta}
\end{figure}

\section{CVD per-head temperature heatmaps}\label{app:diagnostics:cvd}

The CVD mechanism's per-head learnable temperature $\tau_{h}$ is the
free parameter of the chunk-local preconditioner of
Section~\ref{sec:proposed:cvd}. Trained values per layer and per
head indicate the strength of the key-similarity decorrelation in
each head.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/chapter5/F10_cvd_tau_heatmaps.pdf}

\section{Mechanism-level versus operator-level bidirectional adaptation}\label{app:diagnostics:lion-vs-vim}

The thesis instantiates the bidirectional adaptation through the
mechanism-level LION framework of~\citet{afzal2025linear}. Two
operator-level alternatives appear in the related-work chapter:
the Bi-WKV scan of Vision-RWKV~\cite{duan2024visionrwkv}, which
runs the causal RWKV recurrence forwards and backwards and combines
the resulting hidden states; and the forward-and-backward block
pairing of Vision Mamba~\cite{zhu2024visionmamba}, which applies
the same template to selective state-space models. The
operator-level family pays a doubled serial computation per layer
in exchange for retaining the linear-time envelope, while the
mechanism-level LION wrapper pays the quadratic memory of the
parallel attention but does not double the serial computation.
Layer counts and total parameter counts are matched across the
two families: every cell in
Table~\ref{tab:results:bidirectional-comparison-7m} carries the
same six-layer encoder and the same parameter count as its causal
counterpart, with the bidirectional mechanism replacing the causal
scan in place rather than adding parameters.

The reading at 7M is consistent across the family: on the two
backbones that carry native decay (RWKV-6 and Mamba-2), the
mechanism-level LION wrapper improves test CER by
$\Delta = -0.020$ to $-0.037$ over the operator-level alternative
at matched layers, parameters, and budget. On Linear Attention,
the natural LION-LIT mapping has $\lambda = 1$ and no decay; it
loses to VIM by $\Delta = +0.109$. Adding decay through the
LION-S control reverses the result and yields $\Delta = -0.048$.
The pattern matches the decay-as-prerequisite finding of
Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}: the LION wrapper's bidirectional
benefit on Linear Attention is conditional on a decay-bearing mask,
and the controlled LION-S deployment supplies that decay through a
per-token sigmoid that is not present in the underlying causal
backbone.
