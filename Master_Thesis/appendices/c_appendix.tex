\chapter{Complete Empirical Results}\label{app:results}

This appendix records the complete per-cell empirical evidence behind
the matrix figures in Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}. The first table
is the master matrix on LibriSpeech across all reported architecture,
mode, and scale combinations; the second is the Common Voice
cross-distribution comparison sorted by the magnitude of the
delta-of-deltas; the third is the MQAR length-stratified verdict
table; the fourth is the chunked-streaming evaluation per cell.
Throughout the appendix, character error rate is the primary metric.
The single-seed convention of Section~\ref{app:setup:training}
applies. The codebase noise floor estimated during the discovery
phase is $\sigma \approx 0.0014$.

The LIT and S columns of Linear Attention have separate vanilla
references because the two backbones differ structurally: LION-LIT
runs without per-token decay ($\lambda = 1$ throughout), while LION-S
adds a per-token sigmoid decay parameterised by the Gated RFA $\to$
LION-S row of~\citet{afzal2025linear} Table~1. The
$\Delta$ test column for the LA LION rows is therefore taken against
the matched-mode-decay-class vanilla rather than against any single
LA-LION reference. The LA LION-S DHO row is the lowest LA LION-S
test CER on the matrix and beats the P7-style LION-S composition
(LA LION-S $\times$ CVD$\times$MSDC, test $0.1129$) by $\Delta$ test
$-0.014$.

The reading of the master matrix is the subject of
Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}; the central observations are that
MSDC transfers in deficit-proportional ordering across the three
architectures (LA $>$ RWKV-6 $>$ Mamba-2), that DHO produces a
clean BREAK on Linear Attention and a NULL on Mamba-2 under the
LibriSpeech distribution, and that CVD transfers asymmetrically
across the family with the LION-LIT $\times$ CVD cell as the
controlled falsifier that isolates decay as the structural
prerequisite.

\section{Common Voice cross-distribution}\label{app:results:cv}

The Common Voice pilot consists of 16 cells covering the three
architectures across the same per-mechanism rows used at the 7M
LibriSpeech causal scale. Each pilot cell is paired with the
matching LibriSpeech 7M causal cell. The
$\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}}$ column is the CV test CER minus the CV
vanilla test CER for the same architecture; the $\Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}$
column is the LibriSpeech test CER minus the LibriSpeech vanilla test
CER for the same architecture. The
$\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}$ column measures the
task-prior modulation of the mechanism's transfer, with magnitude
indicating how much the mechanism's effect changes between the two
acoustic distributions. Rows are sorted by
$|\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}|$ descending; the
three vanilla rows appear at the bottom by definition.

The Mamba-2 DHO cell is the cleanest example of task-prior modulation
in the table: the LibriSpeech result is a clean predicted NULL
($\Delta_{\mathrm{LS}} = +0.0002$) consistent with selective

that the block-complex extension exercises.

\section{MQAR length sweep}\label{app:results:mqar}

The MQAR cohort is run as a 10-backbone $\times$ 3-length matrix on
seed 42, with verdict, steps to reach 0.5 per-sequence accuracy, and
steps to reach 0.9 per-sequence accuracy recorded per cell. The
verdicts follow the convention of
Section~\ref{sec:experimental-design}: PASS denotes per-sequence
accuracy $\ge 0.99$ at the early-stopping point; PARTIAL denotes
$\ge 0.5$ but $< 0.9$ at the wall-clock budget; FAIL denotes
per-sequence accuracy at or near zero with the loss flat near the
initialisation level; SKIP denotes a cell that was not run because a
shorter-length cell of the same backbone failed; OOM denotes a cell
that was unable to allocate memory at the configured chunk size.
Cell content is verdict followed by steps-to-0.9 in thousand-step
units (kS) where defined.

\section{Chunked-streaming evaluation}\label{app:results:chunked}

The chunked-streaming evaluation reports test CER on a fixed subset
of $500$ test utterances, decoded under three window sizes
$\{2.0, 5.0, 10.0\}$~s and two state policies (reset and carry).
The full-utterance test CER is reported in the rightmost column for
reference. The reset policy clears the recurrent state at every
chunk boundary; the carry policy retains the state across chunk
boundaries. Smaller windows and the reset policy stress the
mechanism's ability to operate without long-context state, while the
carry policy and larger windows approach the full-utterance regime.
The chunked-streaming evaluation is reported per architecture for
the 7M causal cells; the LION mode and 14M scale are not reported
under chunked evaluation.

The pattern across the three chunked-streaming tables is consistent
with the full-utterance master matrix and illustrates two further
points. First, the carry policy at the longer windows tracks the
full-utterance evaluation closely, indicating that the recurrent
state of every reported cell carries useful long-context information
across chunk boundaries. Second, the reset-policy degradation at
the $2.0$~s window is largest on the cells whose vanilla CER is
highest (LA and RWKV-6 vanilla), and is markedly reduced on the
cells with effective MSDC, confirming that input-side multi-scale
local mixing improves the architecture's robustness to short
streaming windows by providing within-chunk acoustic structure
without requiring long-context state.
