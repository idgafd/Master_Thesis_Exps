\chapter{Experiments and Results}\label{ch:experiments}

This chapter reports the cross-architecture transfer matrix for
MSDC, DHO, and CVD. The evaluation focuses on the pattern of
gains across backbones, access modes, scales, and probes rather
than on any single best CER. After stating the experimental
framework, the chapter aggregates the matrix and analyses its
transfer, scaling, bidirectional, cross-distribution, MQAR, and
composition signatures.

\section{Experimental framework}\label{sec:experiments:framework}

The evaluation uses three probes selected by expressivity axis,
not by aggregate-score competitiveness. Full hyperparameters,
dataset construction, and hardware details are given in
Appendix~\ref{app:experimental-setup}.

\paragraph{Probes and data preparation.} The principal probe is
LibriSpeech train-clean-100~\cite{panayotov2015librispeech},
$100$~hours of audiobook-source read English speech preprocessed
into $80$-mel log-spectrograms with $25$~ms window and $10$~ms
hop, followed by $2\times$ acoustic subsampling that brings
post-frontend sequence length to $T \le 500$ at the
phoneme-to-syllable scale relevant to the axis-1 deficit. The
cross-distribution probe is a $100$-hour balanced subset of
Common Voice English~\cite{ardila2019commonvoice}; the
selection protocol and the pre-experimental comparison with
LibriSpeech are summarised in
Table~\ref{tab:librispeech-vs-cv} and detailed in
Appendix~\ref{app:setup:cv}. Both acoustic probes share the same
$29$-symbol character vocabulary (twenty-six lowercase letters,
the apostrophe, the space character, and a dedicated CTC blank
token). The synthetic axis-2 probe is the Multi-Query
Associative Recall benchmark of~\citet{arora2023zoology} with
the canonical sequence-length sweep $T \in \{64, 256, 1024\}$
and the matching stored-pair counts $K \in \{16, 64, 256\}$ that
grow with $T$ to keep the difficulty parameter $K / d$
meaningful at every length. The synthetic probe uses two
disjoint $8192$-token vocabularies for keys and values.

\paragraph{Evaluation framework.} The acoustic probes report
character error rate under full-utterance greedy connectionist
temporal classification decoding~\cite{graves2006ctc}, with no
language-model rescoring or beam search. This omission is
deliberate: it keeps the metric attributable to the encoder
mechanism rather than to an external decoder. MQAR reports
per-sequence accuracy under PASS / PARTIAL / FAIL verdicts: PASS
at $\ge 0.99$, FAIL near zero with flat loss, and PARTIAL
otherwise. Chunked-streaming diagnostics are reported in
Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics:chunked}.

\paragraph{Training budget and parameter match.} All acoustic
cells use a locked 50-epoch budget with AdamW, peak learning rate
$3 \times 10^{-4}$, cosine decay, $1000$-step warmup, and
SpecAugment LD~\cite{park2019specaugment}. MQAR uses up to
$30\,000$ steps with early stopping at validation accuracy
$0.999$. The 7M scale uses six layers with
$d_{\text{model}} = 256$, $H = 4$ heads, and head dimension $64$;
the 14M scale doubles the layer count with the other widths
unchanged. Mechanism cells are parameter-matched to the
corresponding vanilla baseline within approximately two percent;
per-cell counts and the largest single overhead (DHO on RWKV-6)
are reported in Appendix~\ref{app:results}. Reported gains are
not bought by parameter inflation.

All cells are single-seed because the matched 65-cell matrix
exhausts the available compute. We classify each $\Delta$
descriptively rather than as a formal significance test:
$|\Delta| > 0.010$ is reported as the \textit{break band},
$|\Delta| \le 0.003$ as the \textit{marginal band}, and
intermediate values as productive but not break-band. The
thresholds are descriptive heuristics chosen at
order-of-magnitude granularity; the chapter argues effects
through cross-matrix directional coherence and across-scale
reproduction, not through per-cell significance.

\section{Aggregated empirical summary}\label{sec:experiments:summary}

Table~\ref{tab:results:aggregated} aggregates LibriSpeech
test-clean CER for every reported matrix cell. Detailed per-cell
results and diagnostics are given in
Appendices~\ref{app:results} and~\ref{app:diagnostics}.

Relative reductions are baseline-normalised; deficit-driven
ordering is therefore argued in absolute $\Delta$ terms.

The table shows three mechanism signatures: MSDC follows the
local-mixing deficit ordering, DHO breaks most strongly on Linear
Attention, and CVD transfers asymmetrically with the only harmful
bidirectional cell on Linear Attention LION-LIT. The following
sections unpack these signatures.

\section{Transfer pattern on LibriSpeech}\label{sec:experiments:transfer}

Figures~\ref{fig:f1-transfer-pattern}
and~\ref{fig:f2-deficit-ordering} show the causal transfer
pattern at 7M and 14M. The three mechanisms produce three
structurally distinct signatures, each predicted from the
deficit map of Section~\ref{sec:backbones:deficit}.

The Multi-Scale Depthwise Convolution (\textbf{MSDC}) targets
the short-range temporal-hierarchy axis. Across the three causal
backbones at both 7M and 14M, the absolute $\Delta$ ranks
LA $>$ RWKV-6 $>$ Mamba-2, matching the deficit map of
Section~\ref{sec:backbones:deficit}: Linear Attention, with no
native local mixing, takes the largest gain ($-0.0470$ at 7M,
$-0.0390$ at 14M); RWKV-6, whose Time-Mix block carries a
single-step data-dependent token shift, takes an intermediate
gain ($-0.0261$ at 7M, $-0.0352$ at 14M); and Mamba-2, whose
vanilla path includes a native short convolution at $k = 4$,
takes the smallest gain ($-0.0211$ at 7M, $-0.0196$ at 14M).
The ordering reproduces at both scales and the RWKV-6 gain
grows with capacity, consistent with the mechanism recovering
more of the architectural deficit at the larger scale rather
than saturating. The relative-reduction column of
Table~\ref{tab:results:aggregated} differs from the absolute
ordering at 14M (MSDC delivers $-32\%$ on RWKV-6 against $-29\%$
on LA), reflecting the difference in vanilla baselines ($0.1103$
on RWKV-6 versus $0.1359$ on LA at 14M); the chapter argues the
deficit-driven ordering in absolute $\Delta$ terms throughout,
since the deficit map predicts absolute headroom rather than
fractional reduction.

The deficit map predicts that the Damped Harmonic Oscillator
(\textbf{DHO}), introduced in
Section~\ref{sec:proposed:dho} as the block-complex closure of
the real-diagonal transition, reaches furthest on the
architecture whose vanilla transition admits no native
attenuation and no native phase, and is small on the architecture
whose selective scalar transition absorbs the block-complex
extension under the LibriSpeech audiobook distribution. Across
the matrix on LibriSpeech the direction is consistent with this
prediction: Linear Attention takes the largest absolute gain
($-0.0681$ at 7M, $-0.0412$ at 14M), RWKV-6 a small productive
$\Delta$ ($-0.0061$ at 7M, $-0.0026$ at 14M) consistent with its
native per-channel decay covering the attenuation component, and
the Mamba-2 cell shows a small absolute $\Delta$ ($-0.0030$ at
7M, $-0.0046$ at 14M) consistent with the selective scalar
transition absorbing the contribution under this distribution.
The Mamba-2 reading is task-prior modulated rather than
absolute; Section~\ref{sec:experiments:cv} returns to the same
cell and shows that on Common Voice the same architecture-
mechanism pair is productive at $\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} = -0.0226$,
which constrains the LibriSpeech reading to the
LibriSpeech-as-exercised-by-data condition.

Asymmetric across-family transfer is the clearest signature on
the Chunked Value Decorrelation (\textbf{CVD}) row of the matrix.
The largest single-cell $\Delta$ on LibriSpeech sits on Linear
Attention ($-0.0165$ at 7M), the intermediate $\Delta$ on Mamba-2
($-0.0078$), and the smallest on RWKV-6 ($-0.0042$). This
ordering inverts a naive natural-home-in-attention prior, since
the value-decorrelation principle descends from a softmax
construction (Section~\ref{sec:primitives:cvd}) and might be
expected to transfer most strongly to the family member whose
recurrence is closest to softmax attention; instead the matrix
places the largest gain on the no-decay backbone. The structural
reading of this asymmetry follows once the LION-mode falsifier
of Section~\ref{sec:experiments:lion} isolates decay as the
prerequisite for the bidirectional version of the mechanism.

\section{Cross-scale persistence and LION
mode}\label{sec:experiments:lion}

This section checks whether the causal transfer signatures
persist under scaling and whether LION preserves them under
bidirectional access. Figure~\ref{fig:f7-causal-lion} shows the causal-versus-LION comparison; Figure~\ref{fig:f5-cross-scale} of Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics} supplements the cross-scale reading.

The cross-scale comparison preserves the causal-mode pattern at
both scales. The MSDC mechanism gain preserves on Mamba-2
($\Delta = -0.0211 \to -0.0196$) and Linear Attention
($-0.0470 \to -0.0390$) and grows on RWKV-6
($-0.0261 \to -0.0352$); the LA $>$ RWKV-6 $>$ Mamba-2 ordering
predicted by the deficit map of
Section~\ref{sec:backbones:deficit} reproduces at both scales.
The CVD and DHO mechanisms preserve their causal-mode signatures
across scale as well, with CVD asymmetric across the family and
DHO showing the same LibriSpeech direction as in §5.3. No
mechanism exhibits a ranking inversion under the 7M-to-14M
scaling step; the conditional 30M scale is dropped from scope
on this basis. The matrix ceiling at 14M is the Mamba-2
CVD~$\times$~MSDC composition at $0.0618$, with the Mamba-2 MSDC
single-mechanism cell at $0.0631$ slightly above.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/chapter5/F7_causal_lion_mode.pdf}
\caption[Causal versus LION]{Test CER for causal and LION modes
at 7M. Linear Attention is shown under both the natural LION-LIT
mapping (no decay) and the controlled LION-S variant (per-token
sigmoid decay).}
\label{fig:f7-causal-lion}
\end{figure}

The LION-mode matrix reproduces the causal-mode pattern on
RWKV-6 and Mamba-2: all three mechanisms transfer productively,
with the LION wrapper improving the absolute test character
error rate over the causal counterpart (RWKV-6 LION-S vanilla
$0.0859$ versus causal $0.1049$; Mamba-2 LION-S vanilla $0.0853$
versus causal $0.1036$). On Linear Attention the pattern
bifurcates between the two LION variants, and the contrast
isolates the structural role of decay in the bidirectional
setting.
Under the natural mapping per~\citet{afzal2025linear} Table~1,
Linear Attention inherits LION-LIT with $\lambda = 1$
throughout. The unmasked
$\phi(\mathbf{Q}) \phi(\mathbf{K})^{\top}$ row sums then grow
as $O(T \cdot d_{K})$ in the bidirectional setting
(Section~\ref{sec:lion}), and three cells follow: vanilla
LION-LIT lands at test $0.2951$, well below causal LA at
$0.1879$; LION-LIT~$\times$~CVD becomes harmful at
$\Delta = +0.0243$, the only positive $\Delta$ in the matrix,
since the chunk-local preconditioner loses its conditioning
guarantee on an unbounded value accumulation;
LION-LIT~$\times$~DHO remains a clean break-band gain at
$\Delta = -0.1909$ (a $65\%$ relative reduction), since the
block-complex transition introduces decay through its damping
channel $e^{-\lambda_{\mathrm{eff}}}$ rather than through the
LION mask.

The controlled LION-S deployment on Linear Attention adds a
per-token sigmoid decay that is not present in the underlying
causal backbone. The three cells flip: LION-S vanilla recovers
to $0.1381$; LION-S~$\times$~CVD becomes productive at
$\Delta = -0.0070$; LION-S~$\times$~DHO lands at
$\Delta = -0.0393$, the lowest LA LION-S cell. The contrast
between LION-LIT and LION-S on Linear Attention isolates the
structural reading of the column: \textit{decay is the
prerequisite for the value-decorrelation mechanism in the
bidirectional setting}, while the block-complex transition of
DHO is decay-self-sufficient and remains productive in either
decay class. The operator-level bidirectional comparison
reported in Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics:lion-vs-vim}
reinforces the same reading: LION-LIT loses to the
operator-level Vim alternative on Linear Attention by
$\Delta = +0.1093$, while LION-S beats Vim by $-0.0477$,
locating the bidirectional benefit on a decay-bearing mask
rather than on the wrapper itself.

\section{Cross-distribution validation}\label{sec:experiments:cv}

The 7M causal matrix is repeated on Common Voice English (pilot
configuration in Appendix~\ref{app:setup:cv}) to test whether
transfer patterns persist under broader speaker, accent, and
recording variation. The broader acoustic variance is a
pre-specified probe property documented in the dataset
paper~\cite{ardila2019commonvoice}, not a property inferred from
the experimental result. The delta-of-deltas
$\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}$ measures the
distributional modulation of each mechanism's effect.
Figure~\ref{fig:f4-libri-cv} plots $\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}}$ against
$\Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}$ for every non-vanilla cell.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/chapter5/F4_libri_cv_scatter.pdf}
\caption[LibriSpeech versus Common Voice]{$\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}}$
versus $\Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}$ for every non-vanilla cell at 7M
causal. Off-diagonal cells indicate distributional modulation;
the Mamba-2~$\times$~DHO cell moves from $-0.0030$ on LibriSpeech
to $-0.0226$ on Common Voice.}
\label{fig:f4-libri-cv}
\end{figure}

Most non-vanilla cells, nine of twelve, remain within
$|\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}| < 0.020$,
preserving the rank ordering of the LibriSpeech mechanism column.
Within this group, the Mamba-2~$\times$~DHO cell is the
pre-specified one: it tests the task-prior amplification
anticipated in Section~\ref{sec:experiments:transfer}. The
reading keys on a sign-strength change in the underlying
$\Delta$s rather than on the delta-of-deltas magnitude: the
LibriSpeech result sits at the edge of the marginal band
($\Delta_{\mathrm{LS}} = -0.0030$) and the same
architecture-mechanism pair becomes productive on Common Voice
($\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} = -0.0226$).

Three cells exceed the band. The LA~$\times$~DHO and
LA~$\times$~DHO~$\times$~MSDC cells deliver smaller absolute
gains on Common Voice than on LibriSpeech
($\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}} = +0.0331$ and
$+0.0263$), reflecting the absorbed share of the LA-on-LibriSpeech
break-band pattern under the broader distribution. The
RWKV-6~$\times$~CVD~$\times$~MSDC composition shows the largest
amplification in the opposite direction
($\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}} = -0.0335$),
consistent with the multi-dilation receptive-field configuration
matching the broader Common Voice phonetic coverage. The reading
refines the alignment criterion: alignment is between mechanism and
\textit{task-as-exercised-by-data} rather than between mechanism
and architecture in isolation.

\section{Axis-2 isolation via MQAR}\label{sec:experiments:mqar}

MQAR isolates axis~2: the model must retrieve values for queried
keys from a sequence of stored $(k, v)$ pairs, with difficulty
scaling in the ratio of stored pairs $K$ to architectural state
dimension $d$~\cite{arora2023zoology}. The cohort sweeps
$T \in \{64, 256, 1024\}$ with $K \in \{16, 64, 256\}$ across
thirteen configurations. Table~\ref{tab:results:mqar-cohort}
reports PASS / PARTIAL / FAIL verdicts and steps-to-$0.9$.

The cohort separates cleanly by verdict pattern. Vanilla
RWKV-6, Mamba-2, and Linear Attention all FAIL or PARTIAL at
$T = 64$ already, confirming the no-recall baseline of the
diagonal class without explicit interference cancellation.
Adding MSDC or CVD to any of the three backbones sends the cell
to PASS at every reported length, in one to six thousand
training steps. The causal Transformer baseline PASSes at
$T = 64$ and $T = 256$ within twelve thousand steps but
\textit{FAILs at $T = 1024$} within the matched 30-thousand-step
budget, matching the degradation pattern reported by the
original Zoology study~\cite{arora2023zoology}. A plausible
explanation is that vanilla causal attention with sinusoidal
positional encoding must learn a length-aware
induction-style circuit~\cite{olsson2022induction} that selects
one of $256$ stored keys against $T - K = 768$ distractors per
query, and the gradient signal for forming such a circuit
weakens as $K$ grows; the mechanism-augmented linear-time
backbones, in contrast, supply interference cancellation
directly through MSDC's short-range mixing or CVD's chunk-local
key-similarity preconditioner. The empirical result inverts the
standard sub-quadratic-versus-quadratic narrative at the
sequence length where the comparison becomes binding.

DHO FAILs at $T = 64$ on all three backbones, with the longer
lengths SKIP$^{\dagger}$ed by the same propagation rule that
applies to the vanilla cells. The pattern matches the predicted
axis mismatch: DHO extends the function class along the
damped-oscillator sub-axis of axis~1, whereas MQAR exercises
axis~2. Productive transfer of DHO on the matched-axis ASR probe
therefore does not imply gain on MQAR.

\section{Composition behaviour}\label{sec:experiments:composition}

The pre-specified composition cells of the matrix probe whether
mechanism-prior alignment extends to pairs of mechanisms applied
jointly. The matrix populates one productive composition (the LA
DHO~$\times$~MSDC stack, which couples a phase-side and an
input-side mechanism on the backbone with the largest residual
deficit), one composition that saturates to single-mechanism level
(CVD~$\times$~MSDC on RWKV-6, where CVD's value-decorrelation
contribution intersects the residual deficit MSDC already
addresses), and one that stacks marginally
(CVD~$\times$~MSDC on Mamba-2). Figure~\ref{fig:f6-composition}
shows the pattern across the matrix. The reading is directional
rather than a closed-form composition rule, since only three
composition cells are populated.

\begin{figure}[!htbp]
\centering
\includegraphics[width=0.9\linewidth]{figures/chapter5/F6_composition_saturation.pdf}
\caption[Composition behaviour]{Vanilla, best single mechanism,
and pre-specified composition CER per architecture and scale on
the causal matrix.}
\label{fig:f6-composition}
\end{figure}

The CVD~$\times$~MSDC composition pre-specified on RWKV-6 and
Mamba-2 saturates on RWKV-6 ($\Delta$ over MSDC alone:
$-0.0003$ at 7M, $-0.0005$ at 14M) and stacks marginally on
Mamba-2 ($-0.0030$ at 7M, $-0.0013$ at 14M); the Mamba-2
advantage is consistent with CVD's stronger single-mechanism
transfer on Mamba-2 than on RWKV-6
(Section~\ref{sec:experiments:transfer}). The matrix ceiling at
14M Mamba-2 falls on this composition cell at test $0.0618$.
The DHO~$\times$~MSDC composition pre-specified on Linear
Attention stacks productively at both scales: at 7M causal,
$\Delta = -0.0410$ over MSDC alone, since MSDC fills the
absent input-side mixing deficit and DHO independently fills
the absent transition-side phase deficit; at 14M the
composition delivers $\Delta = -0.0183$ over MSDC alone. The
contrast between the saturating CVD~$\times$~MSDC composition on
RWKV-6 and the productive DHO~$\times$~MSDC composition on
Linear Attention is structural: composition stacks where the
two mechanisms target distinct deficits on the architecture's
operator chain, and saturates where the second mechanism
intersects the deficit the first already addresses.

Exhaustive pairwise composition testing is out of scope for the
matched-budget matrix. The pre-specified pair per backbone was
selected to test the criterion's prediction on different-axis
composition (LA: DHO axis-1-phase $\times$ MSDC axis-1-input)
and same-axis composition (RWKV-6 and Mamba-2: CVD axis-2
$\times$ MSDC axis-1). Alternative pairings such as
DHO~$\times$~CVD on RWKV-6 or Mamba-2 are not run here under
the budget constraint and are left for future work.

\section{Synthesis: mechanism-prior alignment}\label{sec:experiments:synthesis}

The matrix supports mechanism-prior alignment in three ways.
First, within the causal LibriSpeech matrix, each mechanism
produces a distinct transfer signature: MSDC follows the
local-mixing deficit ordering LA $>$ RWKV-6 $>$ Mamba-2, DHO
breaks most strongly on Linear Attention, and CVD transfers
asymmetrically rather than following a naive
natural-home-in-attention prior. Second, controlled contrasts
sharpen the structural reading: Linear Attention
LION-LIT~$\times$~CVD is the only harmful cell in the matrix
and isolates decay as a bidirectional prerequisite for value
decorrelation, while the controlled LION-S deployment restores
productive transfer. Third, probe variation constrains the
criterion: Mamba-2~$\times$~DHO becomes productive on Common
Voice, and DHO fails on MQAR, showing that alignment is with the
task structure actually exercised by the data and not with the
architecture in isolation.

The evidence is stronger on the positive-transfer side than on
the full converse / null side. The matrix shows that matched
mechanisms help in the predicted ordering; systematic
null-effect validation requires future multi-seed work along the
converse-axis, and the falsification-protocol extension recorded
in Section~\ref{sec:conclusions:future-work} of
Chapter~\ref{ch:conclusions} commits the framework to a
two-directional protocol as the natural next step.

The criterion is asserted only for the
diagonal-class function family of Section~\ref{sec:bounds}. The
matrix does not test it on diagonal-plus-low-rank architectures
such as DeltaNet~\cite{schlag2021deltanet, yang2024deltanet} or
RWKV-7~\cite{peng2025rwkv7}, where axis~3 mechanisms for
non-Abelian state-tracking become the natural next target.
Chapter~\ref{ch:conclusions} returns to this extension as the
principal future-work direction.
