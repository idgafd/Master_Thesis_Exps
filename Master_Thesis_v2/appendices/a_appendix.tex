This appendix records the configurations used for the empirical
results in Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}: training hyperparameters,
ASR architecture, dataset construction, MQAR setup, and hardware
environment. Implementations are available from the author upon
request.

\section{Datasets}\label{app:setup:datasets}

Three datasets carry the empirical evaluation, chosen for the
expressivity axis each one exercises rather than for aggregate-score
competitiveness.

\subsection{LibriSpeech clean-100}\label{app:setup:librispeech}

The principal acoustic probe is LibriSpeech
train-clean-100~\cite{panayotov2015librispeech}: $100.6$~h of
audiobook-source read English speech at $16$~kHz, using the
official train, dev-clean, and test-clean splits unchanged. The
character vocabulary contains the $26$ lowercase letters, the
apostrophe, the space character, and a CTC blank ($29$ symbols).

Audio is converted to $80$-mel log-spectrograms with a $25$~ms
window and $10$~ms hop, then passed through $2\times$ acoustic
subsampling. Training filters utterances shorter than $0.5$~s or
longer than $20$~s. SpecAugment LD is used only during training;
evaluation uses full-utterance greedy CTC decoding with no
language-model rescoring.

\subsection{Common Voice English 100h}\label{app:setup:cv}

The cross-distribution probe is a $100$-hour balanced subset of
Common Voice English~\cite{ardila2019commonvoice}, selected
because Common Voice contains broader speaker, accent, and
recording variation than LibriSpeech's audiobook protocol.

The subset is sampled from the validated split under the
matrix-wide seed ($42$, Table~\ref{tab:setup:hyperparams}).
Utterances are grouped by speaker identifier; speakers are drawn
uniformly without replacement until the cumulative duration
reaches the LibriSpeech train-clean-100 budget within rounding.
Utterances shorter than $0.5$~s or longer than $20$~s are
filtered, and validated-split utterances without an associated
speaker identifier are excluded from the speaker-stratified
sampling. Dev and test partitions use the released Common Voice
splits restricted to the sampled speaker set. Preprocessing and
the $29$-symbol English CTC vocabulary match the LibriSpeech
pipeline.

Table~\ref{tab:librispeech-vs-cv} of
Section~\ref{sec:experiments:framework} records the
pre-experimental contrast between the two probes.

\subsection{Multi-Query Associative Recall}\label{app:setup:mqar}

The synthetic axis-2 probe is
MQAR~\citep{arora2023zoology}. Each example contains stored
$(k, v)$ pairs followed by query keys whose values must be
retrieved. The sweep uses $T \in \{64, 256, 1024\}$ with
$K \in \{16, 64, 256\}$, disjoint $8192$-token key and value
vocabularies, and unlimited synthetic training data. Cells train
for up to $30\,000$ steps, stopping early at validation
per-sequence accuracy $0.999$.

The MQAR cohort uses two encoder layers with
$d_{\text{model}} = 64$; only the time-mix block varies. The
causal Transformer baseline replaces the time-mix with standard
multi-head attention at $H = 1$ heads of head dimension $64$ and
a channel-mix feed-forward dimension of $256$. Position is
encoded by a fixed sinusoidal positional encoding of dimension
$d_{\text{model}}$ (sin on even channels, cos on odd, with
$\mathrm{max\_len} = 8000$) added to the token embedding before
the encoder stack; ordering is enforced inside the attention
block by a causal mask. All cohort cells use AdamW with peak
learning rate $3 \times 10^{-4}$, $1000$-step linear warmup, and
cosine decay.

\section{Hardware environment}\label{app:setup:hw}

All cells were trained on a workstation with NVIDIA RTX PRO 6000
Blackwell GPUs ($48$~GB VRAM each). Single-GPU training is the
default. The only exception is the 7M LA LION-S DHO cell, which
used 4-GPU data-parallel training to fit the bidirectional
complex-attention scan while preserving the $300$~s audio batch
budget through gradient accumulation.

The software stack uses PyTorch with CUDA. Mamba-2 uses the
official SSD CUDA kernel from
\texttt{state-spaces/mamba}~\cite{gu2024mambacode}; RWKV-6 uses
the reference time-mix and channel-mix implementations from the
RWKV Foundation~\cite{rwkv2024block}.
