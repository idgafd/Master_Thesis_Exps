\chapter{Complete Empirical Results}\label{app:results}

This appendix records the complete per-cell empirical evidence
behind the matrix figures and the aggregated table in
Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}. The first three tables form the
master matrix on LibriSpeech across architecture, mode, and
scale; each row reports test character error rate, test word
error rate, and the corresponding absolute deltas versus the
matched vanilla baseline, together with parameter counts. The
fourth and fifth tables are the Common Voice cross-distribution
comparisons (one on CER, one on WER) sorted by the magnitude of
the delta-of-deltas. The sixth table is the chunked-streaming
evaluation summary. The MQAR cohort with verdicts and
convergence speed is reported in
Table~\ref{tab:results:mqar-cohort} of
Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}. Throughout the appendix, character
error rate is the primary metric and word error rate the
secondary metric; the single-seed convention applies.

The LION-LIT and LION-S blocks of Linear Attention have separate
vanilla references because the two backbones differ structurally:
LION-LIT runs without per-token decay ($\lambda = 1$ throughout),
while LION-S adds a per-token sigmoid decay parameterised by the
Gated Random Feature Attention to LION-S correspondence
of~\citet{afzal2025linear} Table~1. The $\Delta$ test column for
the LA LION rows is therefore taken against the matched
mode-and-decay-class vanilla rather than against any single LA-LION
reference. The LA LION-S DHO row is the lowest LA LION-S test CER
on the matrix and beats the LA LION-S CVD $\times$ MSDC composition
($0.1129$) by $\Delta$ test $-0.014$.

The reading of the master matrix is the subject of
Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}; the central observations are that
MSDC transfers in deficit-proportional ordering across the three
architectures (LA $>$ RWKV-6 $>$ Mamba-2), that DHO produces a
clean BREAK on Linear Attention and a NULL on Mamba-2 under the
LibriSpeech distribution, and that CVD transfers asymmetrically
across the family with the LION-LIT $\times$ CVD cell as the
controlled falsifier that isolates decay as the structural
prerequisite for the value-decorrelation mechanism in the
bidirectional setting.

architectures across the per-mechanism rows used at the 7M
LibriSpeech causal scale. Each pilot cell is paired with the
matching LibriSpeech 7M causal cell. The
$\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}}$ column is the CV test CER minus the CV
vanilla test CER for the same architecture; the
$\Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}$ column is the LibriSpeech test CER minus the
LibriSpeech vanilla test CER for the same architecture. The
$\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}$ column measures the
task-prior modulation of the mechanism's transfer, with magnitude
indicating how much the mechanism's effect changes between the two
acoustic distributions. Rows are sorted by
$|\Delta_{\mathrm{CV}} - \Delta_{\mathrm{LS}}|$ descending; the
three vanilla rows appear at the bottom by definition.

\section{Chunked-streaming evaluation}\label{app:results:chunked}

The chunked-streaming evaluation reports test CER on a fixed subset
of $500$ test utterances, decoded under three window sizes
$\{2.0, 5.0, 10.0\}$~s and two state policies. The reset policy
clears the recurrent state at every chunk boundary; the carry
policy retains the state across chunk boundaries. Smaller windows
and the reset policy stress the mechanism's ability to operate
without long-context state, while the carry policy and larger
windows approach the full-utterance regime. Reported here is a
summary across the 7M causal cells; per-architecture detail is
given in Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics:chunked}.

The summary across architectures preserves the full-utterance
ordering. Two further points emerge. First, the carry policy at
the longer windows tracks the full-utterance evaluation closely,
indicating that the recurrent state of every reported cell carries
useful long-context information across chunk boundaries. Second,
the reset-policy degradation at the 2-second window is largest on
the cells whose vanilla CER is highest and is markedly reduced on
the cells with effective MSDC, confirming that input-side
multi-scale local mixing improves the architecture's robustness to
short streaming windows by providing within-chunk acoustic
structure without requiring long-context state.
