\chapter{Complete Empirical Results}\label{app:results}

This appendix records the per-cell evidence behind the matrix
figures and aggregate tables in Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}. It
includes the LibriSpeech master matrix, the Common Voice
cross-distribution CER and WER comparisons, and the
chunked-streaming summary. CER is the primary metric and WER the
secondary metric; the single-seed convention applies. The MQAR
cohort is reported in Table~\ref{tab:results:mqar-cohort} of
Chapter~\ref{ch:experiments}.

\section{Chunked-streaming evaluation}\label{app:results:chunked}

The chunked-streaming evaluation reports test CER on $500$
held-out utterances under window sizes $\{2.0, 5.0, 10.0\}$~s
and two state policies: \textit{reset} clears the recurrent
state at chunk boundaries, while \textit{carry} retains it.
Table~\ref{tab:results:chunked-summary} summarises the 7M causal
cells; per-architecture detail is in
Appendix~\ref{app:diagnostics:chunked}.

The averaged chunked results preserve the full-utterance
ordering. The 10-second carry setting tracks full-utterance
decoding closely, while the 2-second reset setting degrades most
for the vanilla cells and least for the MSDC-containing cells.
This is consistent with the view that input-side local mixing
improves short-window robustness by supplying within-chunk
acoustic structure without relying on long-context state.
