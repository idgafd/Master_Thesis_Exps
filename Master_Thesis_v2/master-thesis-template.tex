\begin{abstract}
\addchaptertocentry{\abstractname} % Add the abstract to the table of contents

Linear-time recurrent sequence models such as RWKV, Mamba-2, and
Linear Attention now reach production scale on industrial
deployments while sharing a common architectural family. Each
backbone expresses its hidden state as a bounded-dimensional
recurrence; underneath this shared form, the three principal
members of the family leave different residual deficits along
conceptually distinct but empirically interacting axes of
expressivity, including short-range temporal mixing, associative
recall under interference, and the algebraic richness of the
recurrent transition class. This thesis
investigates whether mechanism-level extensions designed against
documented expressivity deficits transfer across the family at
matched compute, and characterises the structural pattern of that
transfer.

The thesis instantiates three mechanisms (the Multi-Scale
Depthwise Convolution, the Damped Harmonic Oscillator, and the
Chunked Value Decorrelation) and deploys each on three causal
backbones (RWKV-6, Mamba-2, Linear Attention) at two parameter
scales (7M and 14M), two access modes (causal and LION
bidirectional), and three probes (LibriSpeech, Common Voice
English, Multi-Query Associative Recall). The resulting
cross-architecture transfer matrix supports the
\textit{mechanism-prior alignment criterion}, a predictive
heuristic stating that larger residual deficits along the axis a
mechanism targets tend to correspond to larger absolute gains,
and that no measured gain is expected when the mechanism's axis
is not exercised by the task.

The binding empirical claim is that on Multi-Query Associative
Recall at sequence length $T = 1024$, every linear-time backbone
augmented with the Multi-Scale Depthwise Convolution or with the
Chunked Value Decorrelation solves the task while the causal
Transformer baseline fails. The result inverts the standard
sub-quadratic-versus-quadratic narrative at the sequence length
where the comparison becomes binding. Speech recognition is
selected as a probe for the short-range temporal-hierarchy axis
rather than as the application domain of the contribution; the
thesis does not claim state of the art on any speech recognition
benchmark and does not assert the alignment criterion outside the
diagonal-class function family.
\end{abstract}
